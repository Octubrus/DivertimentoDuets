I love scales. However, for most young players learning an instrument, scales and exercises are the spinach and broccoli of the music world and they’d rather skip them and go straight to dessert. I wanted to include books in the Divertimento Duets series which address this important part of hand and ear training while hopefully making them more appealing and fun.

This is why each Volume in the Divertimento Duets series has a Scale and Arpeggio Duet book of an appropriate technical level, integrating and complementing the main books. Used together they constitute a complete methodology for the beginner violinist in their first forays towards becoming an accomplished violinist and musician.

When learning through duets, the ear is being trained subliminally to hear intervals, to listen critically and adapt bad intonation accordingly, over time helping the student develop a strong sense of good intonation. Even in these very first exercises, the ear is starting to recognise intervals, which will also serve the student well when ultimately progressing to double-stopped scales.

These exercises also help the student to recognise and understand musical patterns that they will encounter in the pieces they play, so while these are excellent for hand and ear training, they also ultimately help enormously with the development of sight reading skills.

These scale books maintain the Divertimento Duets premise of equal-level parts, allowing both student and teacher to play together (as well as student and student), making them perfect for one on one lessons as well as group teaching situations.
